Wisdom Section

In the ancient Hindu epic of Samudra Manthan, gods and demons churned the ocean to extract the nectar of immortality (amrita). They used Mount Mandara as the churning rod and the serpent Vasuki as the rope. During the process, deadly poison (halahala) emerged first, which Lord Shiva swallowed to save the world. Eventually, treasures like the moon, Lakshmi, and the amrita appeared.

Relatable message: The mind is an ocean—when churned through meditation and reflection, it reveals both negativity (poison) and treasures (clarity, wisdom, success). Embrace the process to declutter and transform.
